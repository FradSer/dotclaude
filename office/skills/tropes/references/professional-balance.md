# Professional Balance

Achieving a "middle ground" between overly colloquial "human" writing and professional, yet often obscure, AI generation. This style aims for **Expert Clarity**: writing that sounds like a professional human expert communicating with peers.

Source: [tropes.fyi](https://tropes.fyi) & Gemini CLI Guidelines

---

## 1. Precise Terminology over Ornate Analogies

While AI often reaches for patronizing metaphors ("Think of it as a highway"), and overly colloquial writing might oversimplify, a balanced professional uses precise domain terms.

**Do:** Use the correct technical term if the audience understands it.
**Avoid:** "A Swiss Army knife for your workflow" (AI) or "A really handy tool" (Colloquial).
**Balanced:** "A versatile utility for [specific task]."

## 2. Information Density over "Chattiness"

Professional writing is often more dense than casual conversation. Avoid the "TikTok" style of one-thought-per-paragraph, which feels like it's talking down to the reader.

**Do:** Group related ideas into coherent paragraphs that build a logical argument.
**Avoid:** Standalone sentence fragments for "punchy" emphasis (e.g., "He published this. Openly. In a book.").
**Balanced:** "By publishing his findings openly in a book, he ensured the methodology was transparent and peer-reviewed."

## 3. Strong Transitions over Filler Phrases

AI uses "Here's the kicker" or "Let's dive in" to signal transitions. Colloquial writing might lack transitions entirely. A balanced approach uses logic-driven connectors.

**Do:** Use words like "Consequently," "Conversely," "Furthermore," or "However" to show the relationship between ideas.
**Avoid:** "Wait, there's more," "Let's break this down," or "Here's the thing."
**Balanced:** "Conversely, the performance overhead of this approach suggests that..."

## 4. Expert-to-Expert Dynamic

Move away from the "Teacher-Student" dynamic (the "Pedagogical Voice") and toward an "Expert-to-Expert" exchange.

**Do:** Assume your reader is competent. Provide evidence and let them draw conclusions.
**Avoid:** "Let's unpack what this really means" or "I'm sure you've noticed that..."
**Balanced:** "Analysis of the system reveals that..." or "The implementation demonstrates..."

## 5. Chinese Technical Writing Balance (中文技术写作平衡)

Avoid both marketing "buzzwords" and overly casual "blog-style" expressions.

**Do (Chinese):** 使用清晰、直指核心的技术词汇。保持逻辑严密。
**Avoid (Buzzwords):** 「赋能」、「助力」、「生态」、「底层逻辑」、「抓手」、「心智」。
**Avoid (Colloquial):** 「干货满满」、「手把手教学」、「这里有个坑」、「真的吹爆」。
**Balanced:** 「支持」、「实现」、「架构」、「其核心原理是」、「由于...导致...」。

---

Remember: The goal is to be **Clear**, **Concise**, and **Accurate**. A human expert doesn't need to perform "humanity" through slang, nor does a professional need to perform "professionalism" through obscure vocabulary.
